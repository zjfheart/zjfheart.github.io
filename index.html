Research

    Jingfeng's research interests lie in trustworthy machine learning, in which he specializes in adversarial robustness.
    His long-term research interest is making artificial intelligence secure for human beings.

Brief Biography

    Jingfeng Zhang is currently a postdoctoral researcher at RIKEN-AIP in Tokyo, Japan.

    He was born in Qingdao, China, on Oct. 08, 1993. During his undergraduate period, he studied at University of Cambridge for two months and University of California, Los Angeles for three months. Finally, he chose Singapore for his Ph.D. study; he had a happy life there with many lovely friends and supportive mentors. He likes Malaysian durian!

    During his Ph.D. study, he had two valuable internship experiences. In 2018, he was interning at IBM Singapore advised by Laura Wynter, working on the "robust ResNet" project. In 2019, he was interning at the Imperfect Information Learning Team at RIKEN-AIP advised by Bo Han, Gang Niu, and Masashi Sugiyama, working on the "adversarial robustness" project.
