Research

    Jingfeng's long-term research interest is making artificial intelligence secure for human beings.
    Jingfeng's ongoing projects are as follows.
    [Background] When we deploy models trained by standard training (ST), they work well on natural test data. However, those models cannot handle adversarial test data (also known as adversarial examples) that are algorithmically generated by adversarial attacks. An adversarial attack is an algorithm which applies specially designed tiny perturbations on natural data to transform them into adversarial data, in order to mislead a trained model and let it give wrong predictions. Adversarial robustness is aimed at improving the robust accuracy of trained models against adversarial attacks.

    What is adversarial training (AT)? Given the knowledge that the test data may be adversarial, AT carefully simulates some adversarial attacks during training. Thus, the model has already seen many adversarial training data in the past, and hopefully it can generalize to adversarial test data in the future.

    AT has two purposes: (1) correctly classify the data (same as ST) and (2) make the decision boundary thick so that no data lie nearby the decision boundary.
  • AT has smoothing effect: AT can natrually mitigate the side effects of imperfect training sets (such as noisy labels or backdoors).
    Understanding the interaction of adversarial training with noisy labels in 2021.
    On the effective adversarial training against backdoor attacks in 2022.
  • The techniques of AT can evaluate/enhance the reliabilities of some tools (such as image denoiser and non-parametric two sample tests)
    Towards adversarially robust image denoising in 2022.
    Adversarial attacks and defenses for non-parametric two sample tests in 2022.
  • AT can be further improved through leveraging some tricks (such as injecting noisy labels, employing collaborations)
    NoiLin: Improving adversarial training and correcting stereotype of noisy labels in 2021.
    Collaborate to defend against adversarial attacks in 2021.
  • I also get involved in the following several projects.
    Bound and method: sampling-based attack for cooperative multiagent reinforcement learning.
    Robust federated recommendation system.
    Improving adversarial robustness via misclassification aware data augmentation.
    WaveFuzz: A Clean-Label Poisoning Attack to Protect Your Voice.
    AdvDDoS: Zero-Query Universal Adversarial Attacks on Speech Recognition Systems.

Brief Biography

    Jingfeng Zhang is currently a postdoctoral researcher at RIKEN-AIP in Tokyo, Japan.

    He was born in Qingdao, China, on Oct. 08, 1993. During his undergraduate period, he studied at University of Cambridge for two months and University of California, Los Angeles for three months. Finally, he chose Singapore for his Ph.D. study; he had a happy life there with many lovely friends and supportive mentors. He likes Malaysian durian!

    During his Ph.D. study, he had two valuable internship experiences. In 2018, he was interning at IBM Singapore advised by Laura Wynter, working on the "robust ResNet" project. In 2019, he was interning at the Imperfect Information Learning Team at RIKEN-AIP advised by Bo Han, Gang Niu, and Masashi Sugiyama, working on the "adversarial robustness" project.
